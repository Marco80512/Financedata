Definition: Capital outlay, means expenditures for the acquisition cost of capital assets, such as equipment, or expenditures to make improvements to capital assets that materially increase their value or useful life.

,

Definition: Revenue is the money generated from normal business operations, calculated as the average sales price times the number of units sold. It is the top line figure from which costs are subtracted to determine net income. Revenue is also known as sales on the income statement.

,

Definition: Expenditures refer to the total purchase price of a good or service. For example, if a company buys a piece of equipment for $30 million and it has a useful life of six years, this is a capital expenditure. Expenses refer to the amount that is recorded for the purpose of offsetting a company's revenue or income.

,

Definition: General expenses are the costs a business incurs as part of its daily operations, separate from selling and administration expenses.

,

Definition: General revenue is the income a government receives primarily from its taxing authority, and not including income from other sources , such as the sale of utility services like water or power, or from the sale of other goods, such as alcoholic beverages.
